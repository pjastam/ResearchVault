The Consensus AI Model Context Protocol (MCP) is a framework designed to integrate large language models with domain-specific knowledge, particularly in academic research. The protocol enables LLMs like ChatGPT or Claude to access peer-reviewed literature and execute custom academic workflows directly within the data.

### Key Findings
* Integrating Consensus via MCP allows LLMs to perform complex academic tasks such as direct searches of peer-reviewed papers and generation of structured research outputs.
* Pre-built and custom skills (academic workflows) can be imported and utilized, guiding systematic processes like curriculum development and literature reviews.
* The system demonstrates the ability to generate comprehensive research guides, categorizing research gaps into methodological, population, and conceptual areas.
* MCP extends beyond specific chat interfaces, functioning across various clients including ChatGPT, Claude Code, Cursor, and VS Code.
* Custom skills can be generated using the 'Create with Claude' feature, tailoring processes and outcomes to researchers' unique needs.

### Methodological Notes
The study utilizes a functional demonstration of the Consensus AI Model Context Protocol (MCP) connection. The methodology involves configuring LLM instances (Claude and ChatGPT) with specific connectors and 'skills' JSON files, executing pre-defined academic workflows or generating custom ones via natural language prompts.
