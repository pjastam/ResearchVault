## Large Language Models in Academic Research

### Overview

This article discusses the integration of large language models (LLMs) with academic research through the [[Consensus AI Model Context Protocol]] (MCP). By connecting LLMs like ChatGPT or Claude to the MCP, researchers can perform complex tasks such as automated literature reviews and gap identification within peer-reviewed data.

### Key Findings

* **Integration Capabilities**: The integration of Consensus AI via MCP enables LLMs to execute academic workflows directly in peer-reviewed papers. This includes generating structured research outputs.
* **Pre-built and Custom Skills**: Academic workflows, or 'skills,' can be imported or created tailored to specific needs such as curriculum development and literature reviews.
* **Demonstration**: The system has been demonstrated to generate comprehensive research guides. For example, an OPV Research Guide categorizes research gaps into methodological, population, and conceptual areas.
* **Versatility**: This integration works across various MCP clients including ChatGPT, Claude Code, Cursor, and VS Code.
* **Custom Skill Generation**: Custom skills can be generated using the 'Create with Claude' feature, allowing researchers to define their specific processes and outcomes.
