## Overview
The laws of thermodynamics and biology, combined with the unique physical properties of Martian dust, create an insurmountable barrier to survival and return from Mars, making human colonization a one-way trip.

## Key Findings
- The journey to Mars is described as a trap due to the high energetic cost of returning against the flow of entropy.
- Martian dust differs physically from Earth's dust because it has never been smoothed by water or wind, resulting in sharp, jagged particles.
- Inhaling Martian dust can cause systemic internal collapse as human lungs and kidneys cannot filter these hard particles effectively.

## Methodology
The argument is structured from a theoretical framework combining principles of physics (thermodynamics, force relationships) and biology. It uses analogical reasoning to compare geological weathering on Earth with Martian dust history.
